This Week in Global Affairs Digest: Key Insights

Global Affairs Digest: Tensions and Strategic Maneuvers: As Israeli demolitions prompt Palestinians to dismantle their own homes in East Jerusalem, the specter of a humanitarian crisis looms with U.N. agencies warning of potential famine in blockaded Gaza. Meanwhile, the U.S. expresses concern over China's industrial overproduction signaling a bid for global dominance, paralleling strategic risks in the increasingly contested South China Sea. Amidst these geopolitical shifts, North Korea seeks to elevate Kim's stature by supporting Russia in Ukraine, while the tech world braces for a downturn with ASML's forecast, contrasting with the inspiring tale of a woman turning her battle with eczema into a worldwide business triumph.

Global Affairs Digest Highlights of the Week

  • Spike in Israeli demolitions forces Palestinians to raze homes in East Jerusalem.
  • U.S. cautions against China's bid for dominance via overproduction.
  • US strategy in South China Sea risks escalation with China
  • Israel's blockade may cause Gaza famine, warn U.N. agencies
  • ASML forecasts dip due to excess factory capacity, not a semiconductor industry collapse.
  • North Korea boosts Kim's image by aiding Russia in Ukraine.
  • Woman's quest to cure eczema spawns global business success
